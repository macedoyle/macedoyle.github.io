MSU Water
Alliance

2024 2-Video Series Client Work

Turning complexity into clarity — without dumbing anything down.

Environmental science is important. It's also, without the right presentation, deeply easy to tune out. The MSU Water Alliance needed videos that could explain two complex topics — PFAS contamination and septic systems — to a general audience that didn't come in with a science background.

The challenge wasn't just accuracy; it was accessibility. How do you explain what "forever chemicals" are, how they move through water systems, and why they matter — in a way that keeps someone watching past the thirty-second mark? How do you make a septic system feel relevant to someone who's never thought about one?

These two videos are my answer. Each one uses pacing, voiceover, and visual rhythm to meet the audience where they are — and leave them genuinely more informed. This is real client work, delivered for a real public science initiative.
