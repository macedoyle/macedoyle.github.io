I'm Kenzie Doyle — a creative advertising designer and media photographer who just finished four years at Michigan State University diving headfirst into the intersection of strategy, storytelling, and visual execution.

My background in Creative Advertising means I don't just make things look good — I make them make sense. I'm trained to think about the brief before I open any software, to understand who I'm talking to and why they should care, and to build visual systems that serve a purpose beyond aesthetics. My minor in Media Photography adds a different kind of fluency: the patience to build a set, to wait for the light, to understand that the difference between a good image and a great one is often invisible until it isn't.

In school, I worked on real clients alongside conceptual campaigns — producing educational videos for the MSU Water Alliance, shooting product campaigns from scratch, redesigning how legacy brands could look if they were brave enough to update. I interned and worked as a videographer for 42pointSEVEN, which meant actual deadlines, actual clients, and actual accountability.

I'm most at home on projects that require me to do more than one thing — to concept and design, to art direct and shoot, to think strategically and then go make the thing. I thrive when the work asks for both sides of my brain.

I'm currently looking for full-time opportunities in creative advertising, brand design, or content production — at agencies or in-house brand teams where the work is thoughtful and the people give a damn about what they're making.
